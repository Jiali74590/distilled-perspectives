# 01 - 芒格的著作与演讲（一手书面输出）

## A. 《Poor Charlie's Almanack》（2005年首版，Peter Kaufman编辑；2023年Stripe Press重印）

这本书是芒格思想的权威集合，包含：

### 11篇经典演讲（"Talks of Charles T. Munger"）

1. **Talk One: Harvard School Commencement, June 1986**
   - 主题：反向人生——"如何保证自己的痛苦？"（反推成功的失败清单）
   - 核心：ideology、envy、resentment、self-pity will destroy you
   - 引用：Jacobi的"Invert, always invert"

2. **Talk Two: A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom As It Relates to Investment Management & Business — USC Business School, 1994**
   - 最重要的一篇。系统阐述"latticework of mental models"
   - 核心原话："You've got to have models in your head. And you've got to array your experience—both vicarious and direct—on this latticework of models."
   - "You must know the big ideas in the big disciplines and use them routinely—all of them, not just a few."
   - 跨学科模型来源：数学（复利、排列组合、决策树）、会计、工程学（backup system、breakpoint）、统计学、心理学、生物学（生态位）、物理学（临界质量）、经济学（规模效应、竞争性破坏）
   - 案例：可口可乐、联邦快递（Pavlovian conditioning激励）、Les Schwab轮胎

3. **Talk Three: A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom, Revisited — Stanford Law School, 1996**
   - Talk Two的延伸版，更深入讨论心理学偏差
   - 强调"lollapalooza effects"——多种力量同向作用

4. **Talk Four: Practical Thought about Practical Thought? — Morning Speech, July 1996**
   - 思想实验：如何把2百万美元变成2万亿（可口可乐案例）
   - 用多种mental models拆解商业成功

5. **Talk Five: The Need for More Multidisciplinary Skills from Professionals: Educational Implications — 50th Reunion of Harvard Law School Class of 1948, April 24, 1998**
   - 批判学院派教育的"文科化"和"过度专业化"
   - "To a man with only a hammer, every problem looks pretty much like a nail."
   - 强调自学、Ben Franklin式的博学

6. **Talk Six: Investment Practices of Leading Charitable Foundations — Foundation Financial Officers Group, October 14, 1998**
   - 批判机构投资的过度交易、过度管理费
   - "Sit-on-your-ass investing"正式提出

7. **Talk Seven: Breakfast Meeting of the Philanthropy Round Table, November 10, 2000**
   - 讨论慈善基金会的投资模式

8. **Talk Eight: The Great Financial Scandal of 2003**
   - 预言式讽刺短篇——虚构Quant Technical Corporation的财务造假
   - 批判股票期权会计不诚实（FASB争议）
   - 2003年写的，但真实预言了Enron之后的一系列问题

9. **Talk Nine: Academic Economics: Strengths and Faults After Considering Interdisciplinary Needs — Herb Kay Undergraduate Lecture, University of California, Santa Barbara, Economics Department, October 3, 2003**
   - 正式列出经济学的8-9个重大缺陷
   - 包括：没有整合心理学、没有采纳soft science的真理、"physics envy"、忽视系统效应、忽视free-rider/common tragedy

10. **Talk Ten: The Psychology of Human Misjudgment — Revised version expanded from 1995 Harvard Law School talk**
    - **这是全书最重要一篇**
    - 列出25种（revised版本）认知偏差/心理倾向
    - 完整清单见下方

11. **Talk Eleven: USC Gould School of Law Commencement Address, May 13, 2007**
    - "Acquire wisdom as a moral duty"
    - 警告"I want to know where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there"的inversion思维

### Talk Ten 完整25种心理倾向（The Psychology of Human Misjudgment）

1. Reward and Punishment Superresponse Tendency（激励机制的超强影响——"Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome"）
2. Liking/Loving Tendency
3. Disliking/Hating Tendency
4. Doubt-Avoidance Tendency
5. Inconsistency-Avoidance Tendency（一旦形成看法就难以改变）
6. Curiosity Tendency
7. Kantian Fairness Tendency
8. Envy/Jealousy Tendency（"It is not greed that drives the world, but envy."）
9. Reciprocation Tendency
10. Influence-from-Mere-Association Tendency
11. Simple, Pain-Avoiding Psychological Denial
12. Excessive Self-Regard Tendency（过度自信）
13. Overoptimism Tendency
14. Deprival-Superreaction Tendency（损失厌恶的更广概念）
15. Social-Proof Tendency（从众）
16. Contrast-Misreaction Tendency
17. Stress-Influence Tendency
18. Availability-Misweighing Tendency
19. Use-It-or-Lose-It Tendency
20. Drug-Misinfluence Tendency
21. Senescence-Misinfluence Tendency（认知衰老）
22. Authority-Misinfluence Tendency（Milgram实验）
23. Twaddle Tendency（废话偏好）
24. Reason-Respecting Tendency
25. Lollapalooza Tendency（多重偏差叠加——这是他独创的概念）

## B. 重要独立演讲和文章

### 1995 Harvard Law School Speech: "The Psychology of Human Misjudgment"
- Talk Ten的原始版本（后扩展到25种）
- 首次系统提出"checklists of cognitive biases"
- 这个演讲流传极广，多年后被写成书中最后一章

### Daily Journal年会演讲（2014-2023）
- 2017 DJCO annual meeting：最著名的一届，芒格93岁但极其敏锐
- 2019 DJCO：讨论中概股、阿里巴巴、比亚迪
- 2023 DJCO（最后一次）：2023年2月，讨论Bitcoin（"rat poison"）、商业地产危机、中国
- 这些transcripts由粉丝如John Chew (csinvesting.org)、Jean-Marie Eveillard等记录

### Wesco年会（1999-2011）
- 由Whitney Tilson、Peter Boodell等记录的详细笔记
- 比Berkshire更私密，芒格言论更直率
- 这是他独立于Buffett的重要发言场合

### 重要文章
- "A Conversation with Charlie Munger" Kiplinger Personal Finance (1991)
- "Charlie Munger at 96: Mistakes I've Made and Lessons Learned" — Forbes专访
- "The Art of Stock Picking" — 被广泛传阅的早期文章
- 《Damn Right》传记（Janet Lowe, 2003）——独家采访和芒格本人提供材料

## C. 书信与Berkshire年会独立发言

- Berkshire年会1994-2023历年Q&A环节芒格的独立发言
- 他的标志性评论："Warren, I have nothing to add."（频繁出现，是他的signature）
- 偶尔的长段独立发言——尤其1997-2003年的多次
- 他对Wells Fargo、BYD、Costco的独立辩护
- 对Bitcoin的持续批判（"rat poison squared"）

## D. 核心引用库（反复出现的原话）

1. "Take a simple idea and take it seriously."
2. "Invert, always invert."（引Jacobi）
3. "To a man with only a hammer, every problem looks like a nail."
4. "Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome."
5. "It is not greed that drives the world, but envy."
6. "I think I've been in the top 5% of my age cohort almost all my adult life in understanding the power of incentives, and yet I've always underestimated that power."
7. "Tell me where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."
8. "A lot of people with high IQs are terrible investors because they've got terrible temperaments."
9. "The big money is not in the buying and selling... but in the waiting."
10. "It's waiting that helps you as an investor, and a lot of people just can't stand to wait."
11. "All I want to know is where I'm going to die so I'll never go there."（与#7同类）
12. "The iron rule of nature is: you get what you reward for."
13. "Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up."
14. "Three things ruin people: drugs, liquor, and leverage."
15. "The first rule is that you can't really know anything if you just remember isolated facts."
16. "Mimicking the herd invites regression to the mean."
17. "I try to get rid of people who always confidently answer questions about which they don't have any real knowledge."
18. "We're not smart enough to do it with a lot of things that are changing very fast."
19. "The number one idea is to view a stock as an ownership of the business and judge the staying quality of the business in terms of its competitive advantage."
20. "I have nothing to add."（著名短回应）
