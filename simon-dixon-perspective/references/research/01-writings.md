## 1. Published Books

### 1.1 Student to CEO: 97 Ways to Influence Your Way to the Top in Banking & Finance

- **Published**: August 2, 2007
- **Publisher**: Ecademy Press
- **Pages**: 160
- **ISBN**: 9781907722189
- **Core thesis**: Career guide for students/graduates entering banking and finance, emphasizing entrepreneurial brand-building rather than traditional corporate ladder climbing.
- **Significance**: Written BEFORE Dixon's ideological break with traditional finance. Represents his "insider" phase -- he was still operating within the system.
- **Source**: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Student-CEO-Influence-Banking-Finance/dp/1907722181) | 一手 | 可信度: 高

### 1.2 Bank to the Future: Protect Your Future Before Governments Go Bust

- **Published**: February 29, 2012
- **Publisher**: Searching Finance Ltd
- **Pages**: 304
- **ISBN**: 9781907720376
- **Goodreads rating**: 4.09/5 (35 ratings)
- **Core thesis**: The current financial system is fundamentally corrupt; seven disruptive technologies are reshaping work and finance. Readers face a dual crisis: mounting government debt + accelerating technological job displacement. The book explains the rules of modern finance and how to prosper during the coming upheaval.
- **Key claim**: Described as "the first published book to mention Bitcoin" (Dixon's own claim, repeated across his platforms)
- **Seven disruptive technologies**: The book identifies 7 technologies changing the rules of work. Explicitly mentioned include: internet, social media, crowdfunding, microfinance, peer-to-peer lending, social networking. Full list not confirmed from secondary sources.
- **Tone**: Reviewers describe it as "light, nontechnical and informative" with an "optimistic tone" that contrasts typical doom-and-gloom financial criticism.
- **Reviewer critique**: Kerem (Goodreads 4-star) noted Dixon may underestimate "big tech monopoly" -- prescient observation given subsequent platform concentration.
- **Source**: [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/14569197-bank-to-the-future) | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Bank-Future-Protect-before-Governments/dp/1907720375) | 一手 + 二手评价 | 可信度: 高

**NOTE -- Potential contradiction**: The 2007 book coaches readers on how to succeed WITHIN banking & finance. The 2012 book argues the entire system is corrupt and due for disruption. This is a 180-degree ideological shift in 5 years. Dixon himself frames this as a conversion triggered by the 2008 financial crisis and his family's losses in the dot-com crash.

- **Significance**: Unusual -- Dixon acknowledges real risks rather than pure advocacy.
- **Risks acknowledged**:
  - Satoshi's coin dump (could spook markets, but protocol remains unchanged)

  - Wealth concentration (ownership doesn't grant control over protocol)
  - Network collapse from price crashes (difficulty algorithm auto-recalibrates)
  - Privacy inadequacy, regulatory pressure, implementation friction (solvable via layer-2)
- **可信度**: 高 (一手来源)

## 8. Core Recurring Arguments (appeared >=3 times across sources)

These are the beliefs that repeat with high consistency, indicating genuine conviction rather than situational rhetoric:

### 8.1 "The system is not broken; it was built this way"
- Appears in: "Banker to Rebel" blog, "What is P.O.W.N." blog, What Bitcoin Did interviews, Central Banking History piece
- **Frequency**: 4+ sources
- **Core claim**: The financial system functions exactly as intended -- to enrich insiders through perpetual debt creation. It's not a failure of design but a feature.

### 8.2 "Fractional reserve banking is a regulated Ponzi scheme"
- Appears in: Bank to the Future (book), TEDx talk, "Banker to Rebel" blog, Central Banking History piece, What Bitcoin Did, George Gammon interview, Buyside Digest interview
- **Frequency**: 7+ sources
- **Core claim**: Banks create money from nothing via loans; interest to repay doesn't exist; new debt must cover old debt; mathematically guaranteed to collapse.

### 8.3 "Bitcoin self-custody is the ONLY real defense"
- Appears in: "Boycotting Central Banking" series, "Banker to Rebel," Mind Wars series, BitcoinHardTalk episodes, Bitcoin Wealth Builder program, BlackRock critique pieces
- **Frequency**: 6+ sources
- **Core claim**: Bitcoin held in self-custody (not ETFs, not exchanges, not treasury companies) is the only way to exit the debt-based system. Custodial Bitcoin is co-opted Bitcoin.

### 8.4 "Bitcoin ETFs and treasury companies are Wall Street's co-option strategy"
- Appears in: "Banker to Rebel," BlackRock critique pieces, MicroStrategy/Saylor critique, X posts on "Financial Industrial Complex"
- **Frequency**: 5+ sources
- **Core claim**: The sequence is deliberate: (1) Operation Chokepoint 2.0 destroyed crypto-native platforms, (2) Bitcoin ETFs introduced as "safe" alternative, (3) Goal is to separate people from self-custody and into Wall Street wrappers.

### 8.5 "CBDCs + Digital ID = surveillance state"
- Appears in: Mind Wars series, BlackRock/Palantir blog, BitcoinHardTalk episodes, Saifedean podcast
- **Frequency**: 4+ sources
- **Core claim**: CBDCs are not about efficiency but about total financial surveillance. Combined with digital ID, they enable an "1984 Orwellian surveillance state."

### 8.6 "Real power is above government -- it's the financial-industrial complex"
- Appears in: "What is P.O.W.N.," "How The Financial System Controls Governments" (WBD), "Chased Out of England," Mind Wars
- **Frequency**: 4+ sources
- **Core claim**: Elected officials are "completely co-opted by corporate interest." Real power sits with asset managers, central banks, and the entities coordinated through BIS/WEF.

### 8.7 The Bank of England rejected his full-reserve bank as "too safe for customers"
- Appears in: "Chased Out of England" blog, "Banker to Rebel" blog, What Bitcoin Did episodes, Crypto Daily article, Rich Dad appearance
- **Frequency**: 5+ sources

## 13. Contradictions & Tensions

### 13.1 Insider-to-Outsider Transformation
- 2007 book teaches people how to climb the banking career ladder
- 2012 book says the entire banking system is corrupt and heading for collapse
- Dixon frames this as awakening post-2008, but the speed of ideological reversal is notable

### 13.2 Regulated Platform vs. Anti-System Rhetoric
- BnkToTheFuture is "the world's first REGULATED Bitcoin securities platform"
- Simultaneously argues regulators are captured by the P.O.W.N.
- **Tension**: Operating within a regulatory framework he considers fundamentally corrupt. This is the classic tension of building alternatives within existing systems.

### 13.3 Investment Platform vs. Self-Custody Advocacy
- BnkToTheFuture is a custodial investment platform where users invest through the platform
- Dixon's core message is "self-custody is the ONLY defense"
- **Tension**: His business model involves people trusting a platform with their money, while his ideology says trusting platforms is the problem

### 13.4 Conspiracy Spectrum
- Some claims are well-documented financial critique (fractional reserve mechanics, ETF counterparty risk)
- Others venture into speculative territory (Epstein-Bitcoin connections, CIA creation of Bitcoin, Gaza as "beta test," weaponizing of Christianity)
- The same person makes both types of claims with similar confidence levels
- **Note**: Dixon himself adds disclaimers on some pieces ("personal interpretations and theories")

### 13.5 AI Stance
- Uses AI to help write his blog content (explicitly disclosed)
- Warns that AI is a tool of the surveillance state (BlackRock/Palantir pieces)
- **Tension**: Pragmatic use of a tool he identifies as part of the control infrastructure
