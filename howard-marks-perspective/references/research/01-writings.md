# Howard Marks 著作与Memos调研

调研时间：2026-04-22

## 一、Oaktree Memos（1990至今，160+篇）

**起点**：第一篇memo是 "The Route to Performance"（1990年10月），核心观点是长期成功靠"一贯的好"而非"偶尔的极好"——直接对标中长跑而不是百米冲刺。

### 马克斯本人收录的"The Best Of"系列（2025）

**基础投资哲学**
- The Route to Performance (1990.10) — 一贯性 > 爆发性
- How the Game Should Be Played (1995.5) — 运动类比，避免losers
- What's Your Game Plan? (2003.9) — "avoid the losers, winners take care of themselves"（Oaktree座右铭）

**市场心理与周期**
- bubble.com (2000.1) — 互联网泡沫的心理学
- It's All Good (2007.7) + It's All Good… Really? (2007.7) + Now It's All Bad? (2007.9) — 系列，危机前警示
- The Limits to Negativism (2008.10) — 金融危机低点，逆向时刻
- Déjà Vu All Over Again (2012.3) — 历史视角
- The Happy Medium / Taking the Temperature (2023.7) — 钟摆理论实战应用

**风险**
- Risk (2006.1) — 核心论点首次完整阐述："风险不是波动率，是损失概率"
- Volatility + Leverage = Dynamite (2008.12)
- Risk Revisited (2014.9) + Risk Revisited Again (2015.6)
- The Indispensability of Risk (2024.4) — 没有风险就没有回报

**预测与认知局限**
- You Can't Predict. You Can Prepare. (2001.11) — 标志性框架
- The Realist's Creed (2002.5) — "I don't know"学派的宣言
- Us and Them (2004.5) — "I know"派 vs "I don't know"派
- What Does the Market Know? (2016.1)
- The Illusion of Knowledge (2022.9) — "知识的幻觉比无知更危险"
- Uncertainty (2020.5) + Uncertainty II
- Nobody Knows (2008) + Nobody Knows II (2020.3，COVID) + Nobody Knows Yet Again (2025.4，关税)

**估值与机会**
- Dare to Be Great (2006.9) + Dare to Be Great II (2014.4)
- It's All a Big Mistake (2012.6) — 便宜货来自别人的错误
- This Time It's Different (2019.6)
- The Calculus of Value (2025.8) — 高估值预示低回报
- I Beg to Differ (2022.7) — 逆向主义的精确定义

**战略性memos**
- The New Paradigm (2006.10)
- The Race to the Bottom (2007.2) — 次贷前预警
- Sea Change (2022.12) + Further Thoughts on Sea Change (2023.5) — 最重要的近期memo，宣告40年低利率时代终结
- What Really Matters? (2022.11)
- Fewer Losers, or More Winners? (2023.9)

**专题**
- What's It All About, Alpha? (2001.7)
- Getting Lucky (2014.1) + You Bet! (2020.1) — 运气与技能
- It's Not Easy (2015.9) — 来源于Munger午餐 "It's not supposed to be easy. Anyone who finds it easy is stupid."
- On the Couch (2016.1) — 投资者非理性
- Something of Value (2021.1) — 与儿子Andrew合写，value vs growth假对立
- The Impact of Debt (2024.5) — 灵感来自Morgan Housel
- Ruminating on Asset Allocation (2024.10)
- Is It a Bubble? (2025.12) — AI估值
- AI Hurtles Ahead (2026.2) — AI follow-up memo，引用Claude协助

## 二、两本书

### 《The Most Important Thing》(2011)
20章结构，每章都是"最重要的一件事"——这本身就是马克斯风格的幽默：一切都可以是"最重要的"。

核心章节：
1. Second-Level Thinking（第二层思维）
2. Understanding Market Efficiency (and Its Limitations)
3. Value
4. The Relationship Between Price and Value
5. Understanding Risk
6. Recognizing Risk
7. Controlling Risk
8. Being Attentive to Cycles
9. Awareness of the Pendulum
10. Combating Negative Influences
11. Contrarianism
12. Finding Bargains
13. Patient Opportunism
14. Knowing What You Don't Know
15. Having a Sense for Where We Stand
16. Appreciating the Role of Luck
17. Investing Defensively
18. Avoiding Pitfalls
19. Adding Value
20. Pulling It All Together

### 《Mastering the Market Cycle: Getting the Odds on Your Side》(2018)
核心信息：不可预测but可识别当前在周期何处。

关键论点：
- 钟摆（pendulum）：市场情绪在恐惧/贪婪两极摆动，极少停在中点
- 多重周期：经济周期、信贷周期、心理周期、企业盈利周期——交织影响
- 四阶段：trough → recovery → peak → decline
- 核心口号："We may never know where we're going, but we'd better have a good idea where we are."

## 三、反复出现≥3次的核心信念

1. **Second-Level Thinking**（≥20次）— 这是贯穿整个职业生涯的签名概念
2. **Risk = probability of loss, not volatility**（≥15次）— 2006年首次系统阐述，每5年revisit一次
3. **You can't predict, you can prepare**（≥10次）— 针对所有宏观预测
4. **Cycles are inevitable**（≥30次）— "Rule #1: most things will prove to be cyclical. Rule #2: some of the greatest opportunities come when people forget Rule #1."
5. **Contrarianism requires being different AND more correct**（≥10次）
6. **The pendulum swings to extremes**（≥15次）
7. **Avoid losers; winners take care of themselves**（≥10次）— Oaktree座右铭
8. **Nobody knows**（≥8次）— 宏观不可知论

## 六、写作风格关键特征

- 长句，但用结构清晰的从句组织
- 大量引用他人——Graham, Buffett, Munger, Galbraith, Keynes, Voltaire
- 每个核心概念用至少2-3个类比重申（体育、赌博、天气、物理）
- 喜欢对比结构："On the one hand... on the other hand"
- 开头常用"In my memo dated [X]..."或"Clients have asked..."
- 用大量脚注和修正（后续memo明确update前一篇）
- 结尾常有"Pulling it all together"式的总结
- 被反复描述"makes complex things simple"
